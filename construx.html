Common Construction Industry Challenges in Daily Operations

Construction companies often struggle with managing multiple projects, controlling material costs, tracking labor and contractors, and maintaining financial accuracy across sites. Without a centralized system, operations become fragmented, leading to project delays, cost overruns, material leakage, and poor decision-making visibility.

Scattered Project & Site Management

Managing multiple construction projects, sites, and phases without a unified system leads to poor coordination, lack of real-time visibility, and difficulty in tracking progress, timelines, and cost centers.

Uncontrolled Material & Purchase Management

Material procurement without proper tracking results in over-ordering, shortages, and price inconsistencies. Lack of supplier comparison and approval workflows leads to higher costs and reduced margins.

Material Leakage & Inventory Mismanagement

Without site-wise inventory tracking, construction companies face material wastage, theft, and misuse. Poor tracking of inward, outward, and consumption creates gaps between purchase and actual usage.

Contractor, Labor & Billing Issues

Manual handling of contractors and labor leads to disputes, over-billing, and delays. Lack of structured tracking for work progress, payments, and retention amounts affects project execution and financial control.

Purchase Management for Construction Materials

Streamline material procurement with site-wise purchase requisitions and orders. Compare supplier rates, track purchase history, monitor deliveries, and implement approval workflows to control material costs.


Construction Inventory & Material Tracking

Track site-wise inventory with complete visibility of material inward, outward, and consumption. Monitor stock movement, transfer materials between sites, and identify wastage, shortages, and excess inventory.

Asset & Equipment Management for Construction

Manage construction machinery and assets efficiently by tracking allocation, movement, and usage. Monitor equipment utilization, maintenance schedules, and idle time to reduce operational losses.

Contractor & Sub-Contractor Management

Manage contractors and subcontractors with structured work allocation and tracking. Monitor work progress, handle billing and payments, and manage advances, retention, and deductions to avoid disputes.


Labor & Site Workforce Management

Track site-wise labor attendance, allocation, and productivity. Monitor workforce costs per project, assign labor to tasks, and analyze productivity across sites for better planning and control.

Construction Accounting, Financial Management & MIS Reporting
